What is Forensic Meteorology?

Forensic meteorology is the science of researching and reconstructing specific weather events for a given date, time, and location anywhere in the world.

Historical weather data can be obtained by a professional forensic meteorologist from a variety of sources including radar and satellite data, weather reports, and even eyewitness accounts in some cases. For example cases of how weather data can be used view this page.

How Historical Weather Data is Used

The data compiled by a Forensic Meteorologist is typically used by lawyers, insurance companies, airlines, home owners, and government agencies.

For depositions and trials, legal professionals use historical weather data and reconstruction reports for discovery, and forensic weather expert witness testimony in trials. Insurance companies ask for similar historical weather reports in claims disputes.
